A relatively simple "phase-space"-like plot for the evolution of COVID-19 cases and deaths

Urbano L. França

Boston Children’s Hospital & Harvard Medical School


Click here to go directly to plots: World | US


The x-axis shows the mean daily growth over a certain period (14 days) normalized by the maximum daily growth, $$x = \frac{(\sum_{14\mathrm{\ days}} \mathrm{daily \ cases})/14 }{\max(\mathrm{daily \ cases}) }.$$ The y-axis corresponds to the contribution of the last period (14 days) to the total of cases, $$y = \frac{\sum_{14\mathrm{\ days}} \mathrm{daily \ cases}}{\mathrm{Total \ cases} }.$$ Sizes are proportional to the number of cases and also by hovering over the curves. Individual countries/states can be removed from the plot by selecting them on the right-hand side panel.
